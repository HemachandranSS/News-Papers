 Volatile Market – Testing Your Patience: A Wait and Watch Game

The Indian stock market in 2024 was a wild ride, with significant ups and downs. Investors needed to be tough and flexible due to factors both within India and around the world. This volatility continues to impact the market in February 2025.

Nifty Breaks 29-Year Record

Nifty's bearish streak is on track to break a 29-year record. With a negative close in February 2025, Nifty will mark its fifth consecutive month of decline, an exceptionally rare occurrence not seen in the past 29 years. The last instance of such a losing streak occurred between July and November 1996, when Nifty closed with a loss of 8.68% in November 1996.

We can draw similarities between the situation then and now. Even in 1996, the Indian stock markets experienced higher price volatility. This was primarily due to tight liquidity conditions, high interest rates, and poor corporate sector results, which were some of the main factors contributing to the depressed sentiment in the stock markets.

Factors that Impacted the Market in February 2025

Global Macroeconomic Factors

  • Persistent inflationary pressures across major economies have prompted central banks to maintain or even increase interest rates. This has slowed economic growth projections and increased borrowing costs for businesses, impacting profitability.
  • Concerns about a potential global recession are growing, as economic indicators in several key regions show signs of slowing down.
  • Rising interest rates and recession fears have led to a shift in investor sentiment, moving from growth-oriented strategies to more defensive positions. This has resulted in increased selling pressure, particularly on stocks with higher valuations.

Domestic Macroeconomic Factors

  • While India's economy has shown resilience, it is not immune to global trends. Concerns about inflation, although moderating, and its impact on consumer spending persist.
  • India's economy grew by 6.2 percent in the December quarter, with FY25 growth projected at 6.5 percent.
  • Foreign Institutional Investor (FII) outflows have contributed to market volatility. When FIIs sell large quantities of stocks, it creates downward pressure on the market. This selling is often attributed to rising bond yields in the U.S. and a strengthening of the U.S. dollar index against all currencies, including the Indian rupee.

Q3FY25 Results Outlook

Earnings estimates are being cut, and the asking rates to justify current valuations are still higher. Unfortunately, we are seeing more downgrades than upgrades, which is a concerning trend. For the quarter ending December 2024, India Inc. saw their topline and bottom-line growth in single digits. If we exclude financials, which performed better than the rest, the growth rate will decline further.

This earnings-valuation gap is a key area of focus and remains a concern for the market, as despite the fall, the broader market, if not large-cap stocks, is still trading at elevated valuations.

Seasonal Market Behavior

March 2025 may see a continued sell-off for at least the first half of the month. Investors, reeling from potential losses, will likely liquidate holdings to offset their tax liabilities before the fiscal year ends. This preemptive action, coupled with heightened market volatility, will discourage immediate reinvestment. The focus will shift to damage control rather than new acquisitions. The urgency to close books and minimize tax burdens will outweigh the appeal of bargain hunting, leading to a temporary market downturn.

Nifty 50 Outlook

At the onset of February, the Nifty 50 commenced trading around the 22,400 level, subsequently encountering resistance near 23,800. Presently, the index is experiencing significant downward pressure, having breached the critical support level of 22,770. This bearish sentiment, pervasive across the broader market, has contributed to the index's overall weakness. Looking forward, the immediate support for the Nifty 50 is projected around 21,850. Should this level fail to hold, the index could potentially descend further to 21,150. Given the current momentum, the likelihood of a reversal appears minimal, suggesting a continuation of the bearish trend. From the current position, the initial resistance for the Nifty 50 is identified at 22,777. A successful breach of this level could pave the way for a move toward the secondary resistance level of 23,280. However, the prevailing market dynamics indicate that the path of least resistance remains downward, with bearish sentiment dominating trading activity.

Key Market Performance in February 2025

All the key indices closed in the negative, with the BSE Sensex being the least negative, down by 3.73 percent, while the BSE 100 decreased by 4.75 percent. The BSE 150 Mid Cap and BSE 250 Small Cap indices fell by 8.34 and 10.42 percent, respectively. The BSE 500 declined by 5.88 percent. This month, the Sensex managed to contain its losses compared to the BSE 100, BSE 150 Mid Cap, BSE 250 Small Cap, and BSE 500 indices.

Conclusion

The Indian stock market remains under significant pressure as February 2025 closes, with Nifty on track to break a 29-year record for five consecutive months of declines. Persistent global economic headwinds, inflationary concerns, and geopolitical uncertainties have kept volatility high. Domestically, while India’s economy continues to grow, FII outflows, rising US bond yields, and sectoral weaknesses have fuelled market pessimism.

As investors navigate these challenging conditions, the focus shifts towards defensive strategies and capital preservation. With a potential sell-off looming in March due to tax-related liquidations, caution remains key. While opportunities exist in select outperforming stocks and sectors, market sentiment suggests that a sustainable recovery may take time. Patience and a disciplined investment approach will be crucial to weathering this volatile phase. Market volatility is a normal part of the investment cycle, and while short-term fluctuations can be unsettling, it's crucial to maintain a long-term perspective.
