Market Recovery: Genuine Rebound or Temporary Relief?

After months of sustained selling and bearish sentiment, April 2025 brought a much-needed breather for Indian equity markets. The bigger question now is whether this marks the beginning of a lasting trend reversal, or merely a short-lived bounce within a broader downtrend.

Investor sentiment improved noticeably in April, as benchmark indices—including the Nifty 50—staged a sharp rebound. Following five consecutive weak months through February, early signs of recovery emerged in March and continued into April. This momentum was supported by easing global yields, stronger domestic macroeconomic data, and selective buying in oversold sectors.

April’s bounce offers both relief and a moment for reflection. It presents an ideal opportunity for investors to reassess their strategies and rebalance portfolios. While volatility may persist due to ongoing geopolitical tensions, the broader outlook appears to be gradually turning more positive.

Nifty Bounces Back After Breaking a 29-Year Record

The Nifty broke a 29-year record in February 2025 by posting five consecutive months of losses—an unusually rare streak last seen in 1996. At that time, the index had fallen by 8.68% in November, following a prolonged downtrend driven by tight liquidity, high interest rates, and weak corporate earnings—conditions strikingly similar to those prevailing today.

In 1996, the Nifty rebounded sharply, rising by around 21% over the subsequent three months. A similar recovery pattern appears to be unfolding now. After closing higher in March, the index had gained 8.65% by 25 April and is on track to finish a second consecutive month in the green.

Where the market heads from here will depend on several key factors, which we explore in the sections that follow.

Donald Trump’s Tariff Effect and Trade Talk

In early April, the United States, under President Donald Trump, imposed a 26% tariff on Indian exports, citing high import duties on American goods. This move sparked renewed trade tensions and increased policy uncertainty. However, by mid-April, the U.S. announced a 90-day suspension of the tariffs to allow for negotiations—a sign of willingness to resolve issues diplomatically.

Following this shift, U.S. Vice President JD Vance visited India in late April and met with Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Their discussions underscored a shared commitment to strengthening strategic and economic ties, particularly in defence, technology, and energy. The visit raised hopes for a broader trade deal that could ease tensions and stabilise India’s external economic landscape.

Affirmative Action by RBI Boosts Banking Optimism

India’s banking sector, which carries significant weight in frontline indices, witnessed a strong rally in April as the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) introduced a series of growth-supportive measures. The Bank Nifty surged to record highs, driven by consecutive rate cuts, regulatory relaxations, and liquidity-boosting initiatives from the RBI. In a major step, the central bank allowed banks to allocate less towards sovereign bonds—a move expected to free up nearly ₹3 lakh crore for fresh lending and improve liquidity coverage ratios by 600 basis points. This infusion comes at a crucial time, amid early signs of slowing credit growth, and is projected to provide a much-needed boost to both bank lending and overall economic activity.

In addition, the RBI implemented its second consecutive policy repo rate cut in April, lowering it by 25 basis points to 6 per cent. The policy stance was shifted from “neutral” to “accommodative,” signalling the central bank’s commitment to supporting growth amid global uncertainties. To further ease liquidity conditions, the RBI undertook large-scale bond purchases and forex swaps, injecting over USD 70 billion into the banking system.

In a supportive regulatory tweak, the RBI also reduced risk weights on bank lending to NBFCs and microfinance institutions—a technical adjustment expected to release additional capital for banks and reduce borrowing costs for smaller borrowers. With major banks already passing on the repo rate cuts through reduced lending rates, sectors such as housing and automobiles are likely to benefit from cheaper loans, potentially stimulating consumer demand.

Looking ahead, sustained monetary support, coupled with stable inflation, could help maintain the banking-led momentum in the markets.

Geopolitical Tensions Escalate Following Pahalgam Attack

On 22 April, a devastating terrorist attack in Pahalgam, Jammu & Kashmir, resulted in the deaths of 26 civilians, mostly Hindu tourists—one of the deadliest incidents in the region in recent memory. India attributed the attack to Pakistan-based militant groups and responded with measures including the suspension of the Indus Waters Treaty and the expulsion of Pakistani diplomats.

As tensions escalate, there is anticipation that India may launch a retaliatory response. The scale and duration of any such retaliation will be critical. A limited and brief action is unlikely to affect markets significantly; however, prolonged conflict could negatively impact the market in the medium term.

Q4 FY25 Earnings Review

As of 24 April 2025, early Q4 FY25 earnings reflected varied sectoral performances influenced by global macroeconomic challenges and domestic dynamics. The banking sector showed resilience, with strong profitability and improving asset quality, supported by RBI initiatives. Housing finance companies also reported robust growth in assets under management and profitability, reflecting sustained demand.

Conversely, the IT sector grappled with margin pressure despite steady revenue growth, affected by soft global demand and tariff uncertainties. FMCG companies such as Nestlé and HUL faced margin compression due to inflation and sluggish volume growth, although they remain cautiously optimistic about a recovery.

The transmission sector, however, delivered solid revenue growth and expanding order books, indicating positive momentum in government spending. Overall, while earnings have been mixed across sectors, the strength in banking, housing finance, and transmission sectors offers hope for broader recovery. Stock selection will be increasingly important amid these divergences.

Further earnings announcements in the coming weeks are expected to show continued divergence. Sectors like automobiles, consumer durables, and telecom may exhibit varying recovery levels. Auto firms could benefit from rising demand, whereas durables may face inflation-driven headwinds. Telecom companies might see steady growth driven by higher ARPUs and potential pricing power.

Nifty 50 Outlook

After dipping below 22,000 following tariff-related announcements, the Nifty 50 index showed notable resilience throughout the remainder of April 2025. It recovered from early weakness and transitioned into a bullish trend. After a challenging start to the month, the index surged past the critical 23,800 resistance level, briefly testing the 24,450 mark before entering a phase of consolidation. Strong participation from domestic investors, coupled with improved macroeconomic indicators and easing global risks, helped support the buying momentum.

Currently, the Nifty 50 is hovering near the 24,300 mark—a key psychological level. The index is encountering stiff resistance in this range, with traders closely watching the 24,700–24,840 zone as the next potential target. If the buying pressure persists and the index holds above 24,000, a move towards the 25,150–25,250 region could materialise in the medium term.

Conversely, a pullback could occur if the 24,100–24,300 resistance band holds firm. In that scenario, immediate support levels to monitor would be 23,800 and 23,500. A break below these could signal a deeper correction, potentially testing the 23,200 or even the 23,000 level.

Looking ahead, the Nifty 50 is at a crucial juncture. Sustaining momentum above 24,300 will be vital for any continued upside. However, failure to overcome resistance could prompt a short-term correction. Investors should remain attentive to key economic data and upcoming corporate earnings, which are likely to shape market sentiment in the coming weeks.

Conclusion

April 2025 marked a welcome recovery for Indian equity markets, offering relief after a prolonged bearish phase. Key indices, such as the Nifty 50, rebounded—bringing cautious optimism back to the investing community. This turnaround was supported by improving macroeconomic data, easing global concerns, and progress in trade relations, particularly between India and the United States.

Nevertheless, uncertainties persist. Geopolitical tensions and inflation remain significant risks. While sectors like banking and financial services have shown resilience, others—such as metals and IT—continue to face headwinds. Market sentiment has also been influenced by foreign and domestic institutional flows, contributing to continued volatility.

Looking ahead, much depends on the sustainability of this recovery. The Nifty 50’s ability to breach key resistance levels will be closely monitored. As corporate earnings are released, sharper sector-specific trends may emerge, providing new insights into potential investment opportunities.

For now, the message is clear: stay vigilant, reassess your portfolio, and maintain a cautious stance. The market may be showing signs of recovery, but it is not out of the woods yet.
